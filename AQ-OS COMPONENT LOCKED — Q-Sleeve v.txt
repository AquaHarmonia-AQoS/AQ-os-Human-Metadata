# Research Framework: Calibrating Human Intuition via Simulated Authority Decay in LLM-Powered Systems

## 1. Core Concept: Human Intuition under Simulated Authority Decay

This framework explores how **human intuition**—rapid, non-conscious judgment—is activated and guided when the perceived reliability, structure, or correctness of an external system (an LLM) is intentionally and transparently reduced, a process termed **Simulated Authority Decay**.

* **Goal:** To mitigate **automation bias** and foster **calibrated collaboration** by transforming inevitable LLM failures (hallucinations, inconsistencies) into productive signals that engage the user's critical reasoning and domain expertise.
* **Axiom (from user context):** If regret is immutable, store as seed of empathy.
* *Interpretation:* If system failure (authority decay) is inevitable, it must be used as a signal (a "seed of empathy" or caution) to enhance the user's intuitive response.



## 2. LLMs as a Source of Authority and Decay Mechanisms

In high-stakes domains (e.g., medical diagnostics, complex coding), the LLM functions as a system of authority. Decay occurs when this authority is perceived as unreliable.

| Mechanism of Decay | How it Manifests in an LLM | Impact on Human Intuition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Hallucination** | Generates confidently false or factually incorrect information. | Intuition is stressed; forced to question internal judgment against high external confidence. |
| **Inconsistency** | Provides different answers to similar prompts or contradicts previous output. | Intuition is undermined; prevents the user from forming a reliable mental model of the system's "rules." |
| **Vagueness/Ambiguity** | Returns probabilistic or hedge-heavy outputs, leading to choice overload. | Intuition is overloaded; forced to weigh ill-defined options without definitive external judgment. |

## 3. The Solution: AI Confidence Score Decomposition

To manage and regulate authority decay, the unified confidence score must be decomposed into cognitively relevant axes of uncertainty, reflecting how a human expert analyzes a problem.

### Confidence Score Decomposition (Medical Diagnostics Context)

| Decomposed Confidence Axis | Meaning and Origin of Decay | Perceptual Cue Category |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Source Confidence (Epistemic Decay)** | Uncertainty regarding the accuracy, recency, or validity of the supporting literature/facts. | **Provenance** |
| **Data Confidence (Aleatoric Decay)** | Uncertainty regarding the completeness, quality, or reliability of the input data (e.g., missing patient labs). | **Completeness** |
| **Inference Confidence (Model Decay)** | Uncertainty regarding the model's ability to connect the facts and data to the conclusion (internal reasoning). | **Ambiguity** |

## 4. Architectural Implementation: Perceptual Cues

The decomposed confidence scores must be translated into multi-sensory and localized perceptual cues to enable rapid, System 1 intuitive processing, guiding the human to activate System 2 critical reasoning.

### A. Visual Cues for Global Authority State

Visual cues leverage color, shape, and contrast to communicate the overall state of authority.

| Confidence/Decay State | Perceptual Cue Category | Specific Design Elements | Cognitive Impact |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **High Confidence (Stable Authority)** | **Clarity & Certainty** | *Text:* Solid, high contrast. *Border:* Sharp, defined edges. *Iconography:* Solid checkmark. | Signals: "Rely on this; brief review needed." Reduces cognitive load. |
| **Medium Confidence (Intentional Decay)** | **Vagueness & Tentativeness** | *Text:* Slightly lighter, italicized. *Border:* Dotted or dashed lines. *Iconography:* Yellow caution triangle. | Signals: "Proceed with caution; critical review required." Engages deliberate reasoning. |
| **Low Confidence (Near-Zero Authority)** | **Fragmentation & Alert** | *Text:* Light gray, transparent box. *Border:* Fragmented or broken, possibly red tint. *Iconography:* Question mark or broken chain link. | Signals: "Warning; treat as hypothesis only." Triggers immediate override or data gathering. |

### B. Localized Cue Translation and Action Mapping

Cues are applied directly to the part of the output that corresponds to the source of the uncertainty, directing intuitive focus.

| Decomposed Confidence Axis | Perceptual Cue Translation | Action Triggered in User Intuition |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Source Confidence (Provenance)** | A footnote with a grayed-out link and a small hourglass icon. | **Check Source Validity:** Intuition directed to verify the age, author, and journal of supporting evidence. |
| **Data Confidence (Completeness)** | A section listing missing lab values has a flickering red outline or a jittery display. | **Gather More Data:** Intuition directed to the gaps in the patient record; triggers decision for new tests. |
| **Inference Confidence (Ambiguity)** | The diagnosis name is rendered in a slightly blurry font with multiple color tints. | **Consider Alternatives:** Intuition directed to generate and explore other differential diagnoses (divergent thinking). |

## 5. Validation Protocols: Measuring Cognitive Impact

Validation must measure not just performance, but the quality of the cognitive process and intuitive response under simulated decay.

* **The Calibration Test (Trust/Reliance):** Measures the drop in Acceptance Rate (A/R) from High Confidence Cues (HCC) to Low Confidence Cues (LCC) to confirm successful decay regulation. Key metric is the **Miscalibration Error** (accepting false positives or rejecting true positives).
* **The Cognitive Load Test (Intuitive Processing):** Measures **Decision Latency** and subjective cognitive load (e.g., NASA-TLX) to ensure perceptual cues are faster and less burdensome than numerical or text explanations.
* **The Error-Seeding Test (Decomposed Usefulness):** Participants receive targeted errors corresponding to a specific cue (Source, Data, or Inference decay). Key metric is **Verification Alignment**: the percentage of users whose next action matches the intended action guided by the cue.
* **Qualitative Assessment:** Post-session interviews to capture the user's **intuitive narrative** and confirm alignment between the visual/perceptual language and their internal mental model of uncertainty.

This framework outlines the theoretical basis, the design architecture, and the necessary validation for integrating the principle of "human intuition under simulated authority decay" into LLM systems for enhanced safety and collaboration.
